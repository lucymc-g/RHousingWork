Larimer County, Colorado Regional Housing Needs Survey

Analysis of 1,146 responses to the 2025 Larimer County Resident Housing Survey, covering housing choice, stability, displacement, future housing plans, commute patterns, and community views on housing policy and solutions. Produced as part of a regional Housing Needs Assessment for Larimer County, Fort Collins, and Loveland.

Primary Findings by Theme

Current Housing Situation
  • Precariously housed respondents reported the highest rates of poor or fair housing conditions (38%), followed by single parents (35%).
  • 19% of respondents with a disability reported their home does not meet accessibility needs. Ramps and grab bars were most commonly needed.
  • 42% of respondents needing repairs said they haven't completed them because they can't afford to.
Stability & Displacement
  • Rental increases were the most common cause of displacement (35%). Over half of those displaced ended up paying higher rents afterward.
  • Precariously housed (53%) and subsidized housing residents (51%) had the highest displacement rates by tenure.
  • Hispanic and Multiracial respondents were more likely to report being told a unit was available by phone but unavailable in person.
Housing Policy & Solutions
  • Respondents rated "housing affordable to public service and retail workers" as the highest priority housing type countywide.
  • Strong support for light-density infill: 71% found duplexes appropriate in their neighborhood; 64% supported family-occupied ADUs.
  • 60% would allow duplexes or townhomes in their neighborhood; 59% supported higher property taxes on high-value homes.

Data & Methods

Approach

Survey Design

Online snowball sample survey promoted in English and Spanish via social media, flyers, newsletters, and community events (September–November 2025).

Sample

1,146 total respondents. 47% Fort Collins, 42% Loveland, 9% elsewhere in Larimer County, 2% in-commuters. Demographic profile closely mirrors county ACS estimates.

Race/Ethnicity

Combined race/ethnicity variable constructed in R using multi-select race columns and a separate Hispanic ethnicity indicator.

Small Samples

Results for groups with n < 40 are described as suggestive rather than conclusive.

Analysis

Crosstabulations by jurisdiction, tenure, household type, race/ethnicity, and income. Produced in R using readxl, dplyr, and ggplot2.

Context

Produced for the 2025 Larimer County Regional Housing Needs Assessment in partnership with Larimer County, the City of Fort Collins, and the City of Loveland.
